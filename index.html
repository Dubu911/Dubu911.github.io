About Me

I'm Daeyoung, an EECS undergraduate at UC Berkeley with a background as a professional analog artist (watercolor, oil, acrylic, graphite). My interests lie at the intersection of computer graphics and human–computer interaction, where I explore systems that make visual creation more expressive, accessible, and efficient.

I pivoted from studio art after hands-on experience in a semiconductor fabrication plant, where I became fascinated by the elegance of engineered systems. That perspective, seeing aesthetic and algorithmic beauty as complementary, guides my work.

At Berkeley I've built a foundation in algorithms, computer systems, and interactive software. I develop tools and experiences across creative and technical domains:

  • Interactive graphics tools and authoring systems (real-time shaders, procedural materials, visual workflows)
  • Creative software for artists and designers (interaction design, workflow optimization, intuitive interfaces)
  • Computational aesthetics and visual simulations (perceptual systems, artistic rendering, generative methods)
  • Exploratory projects that capture my curiosity (game development, data visualization, predictive modeling)

I aim to design and build tools, interfaces, and rendering techniques that help people create and communicate visually. I bring the rigor of engineering and the sensibility of a practicing artist. I care about both latency and line quality, both algorithmic efficiency and aesthetic impact.

Keywords: interactive computer graphics, real-time rendering, creativity support tools, authoring interfaces, perceptual evaluation, machine learning, game development

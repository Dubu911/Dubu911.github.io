Watercolor Simulation Tool

I built this digital watercolor painting tool in Godot Engine to simulate realistic watercolor behavior. It uses GPU compute shaders to handle fluid dynamics in real time.

The simulation implements water flow, pigment transport, deposition, and evaporation. I wrote custom GLSL shaders that run entirely on the GPU, which keeps everything fast and responsive.

The tool supports pressure sensitivity for tablets, lets you adjust physics parameters in real time, and includes features like pigment lifting and wet-on-wet blending that mimic how watercolor actually works on paper.

Get Fit or Quit üí™

I built this arcade game during CalHacks as a fun take on health and fitness. The goal is simple: dodge junk food and catch healthy meals to survive 60 seconds while keeping your fitness level above zero.

The game gets progressively harder at 25, 40, and 50 second marks with more falling objects. I added visual feedback where your character actually grows bigger as your fitness decreases, which makes the challenge even more intense.

Built with Python and Pygame, it features simple arrow key controls and a straightforward food system. Healthy foods like salad and chicken boost your fitness by 3%, while junk food like cake and ice cream can damage it by 10-15%. The real challenge is staying fit long enough to win.

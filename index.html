About Me

I'm Daeyoung, an EECS undergraduate at UC Berkeley with a background as a professional analog artist (watercolor, oil, acrylic, graphite). My research interests are at the intersection of computer graphics and human–computer interaction. I care about systems that make visual creation more expressive, accessible, and efficient.

I pivoted from studio art after hands-on experience in a semiconductor fabrication plant, where I became fascinated by the elegance of engineered systems. That perspective, seeing aesthetic and algorithmic beauty as complementary, guides my work.

At Berkeley I have built a foundation in algorithms, computer systems, and interactive software. Recently I implemented a watercolor simulation in the Godot engine, wrote a research-style report that cites prior work and explains the algorithms I used, and recorded a short demo showing the tool in use. I am exploring problems such as:

  • Interactive graphics tools that shorten the path from intent to image (real-time shaders, procedural materials, differentiable pipelines).
  • Creativity support and HCI for artists and designers (interaction techniques, evaluation of authoring workflows, learnability).
  • Computational aesthetics (perceptual metrics, layout and brush simulation, generative methods grounded in user studies).

My goal in graduate study is to design and evaluate tools, interfaces, and rendering and authoring techniques that help people create and communicate visually. I bring the rigor of engineering and the sensibility of a practicing artist. I care about both latency and line quality, both user studies and shader graphs.

Keywords: interactive computer graphics, real-time rendering, creativity support tools, authoring interfaces, perceptual evaluation, generative methods
